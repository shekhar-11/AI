So basically ANN (artificial neural networks) are for supervised learning tasks.like generally for classification and regression problems. ANN is a computational model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. It consists of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process and transmit information.



Secondly we have CNN (convolutional neural networks) which are a type of ANN specifically designed for processing structured grid-like data, such as images,video frames.
CNNs are particularly effective for tasks like image recognition and classification due to their ability to capture spatial hierarchies and local patterns in the data.



Next we have RNN (recurrent neural networks) which are a type of ANN designed for processing sequential data, such as time series, natural language, and speech. RNNs have feedback connections that allow information to persist across time steps, making them suitable for tasks like language modeling and sequence prediction.
